Cello Sizing Guide

To determine the approximate correct size cello your child needs:

  1. Seat the child so that the knees are bent at a ninety degree angle.
  2. With the endpin of the cello set at around 12 inches and the cello at a 45 degree angle, the instrument should rest such that the upper rim of the cello body rests on the sternum, and thw left knee contacts the curve below the lower bout corner.
  3. The C-string peg (for the thickest string) should be near the left ear, with the neck a few inches away from the shoulder, and the left hand able to reach both ends of the fingerboard with ease.
